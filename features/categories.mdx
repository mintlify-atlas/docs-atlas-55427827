title: "Categories"
description: "Organize your transactions with custom spending categories."
---

Categories are labels you assign to transactions to group related spending and income together. Examples include `Food`, `Transport`, `Salary`, or `Utilities`. Every transaction requires a category, and categories appear throughout the app — in transaction filters and in the Dashboard's spending charts.

## Viewing categories

The Categories page lists all your categories in a table. Each row shows the category name along with **Edit** and **Delete** action buttons.

If you have not created any categories yet, the table shows an empty state prompting you to create your first one.

## Adding a category

Click **+ New Category** in the top-right corner to open the creation modal.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enter a name">
    Type the name for your new category in the **Name** field. The name cannot be empty.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save** to create the category. The table refreshes automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

To close the modal without saving, click **Cancel** or press `Escape`.

## Editing a category

Click the **Edit** button next to a category to open the edit modal. The current name is pre-filled in the input field. Update the name and click **Save** to apply the change.

Renaming a category updates its name everywhere it is used, including on existing transactions.

## Deleting a category

Click the **Delete** button next to a category to open a confirmation dialog. The dialog shows the category name and warns that the action cannot be undone.

- Click **Delete** to permanently remove the category.
- Click **Cancel** to go back without deleting.

<Warning>
  Deleting a category removes it from the categories list. Transactions that were assigned to that category will retain the category name as a string, but the category will no longer appear in filter dropdowns or spending charts.
</Warning>

## How categories are used

Categories connect several parts of the app together:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Transaction form" icon="file-pen">
    When creating or editing a transaction, you select a category from a dropdown populated by your categories list.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Transaction filters" icon="filter">
    On the Transactions page, the Categories filter lets you view transactions belonging to a specific category.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Dashboard charts" icon="chart-pie">
    The top-categories and all-categories charts on the Dashboard group expenses by category to show your spending breakdown.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Spending insights" icon="magnifying-glass-chart">
    Categories make it easy to spot trends — for example, noticing that `Dining` consistently appears in your top five spending categories.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
  Create categories before adding transactions. The transaction form requires at least one category to exist before you can save a new transaction.
</Tip>
